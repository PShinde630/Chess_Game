Chess Piece Descriptions

  1. King: Moves one square in any direction (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally). The king's objective is to avoid checkmate, where it cannot escape capture.
  2. Queen: Moves any number of squares in any direction (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally). It is the most powerful piece on the board.
  3. Rook: Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically. Each player starts with two rooks, positioned at the corners of the board.
  4. Bishop: Moves any number of squares diagonally. Each player starts with two bishops, one on light squares and one on dark squares.
  5. Knight: Moves in an L-shape: two squares in one direction (horizontal or vertical) and then one square perpendicular, or vice versa. Knights can jump over other pieces.
